Information We Collect

What personal information do we collect from you when you use the Lalo app?
Personal information is information that can be used on its own or with other information to identify, contact, or locate a person, such as name, email address, and where a person goes to school. When registering for the Lalo app, we ask you to enter a unique username and password to identify your profile and differentiate you from other users. We ask you provide an email address for password reset requests, and any other necessary communication. We ask you to tell us the school you identify with so that we can provide the best and most relevant student events in your area. We allow users to optionally share their first and last name. And we receive other data from or about the mobile phone or other devices you use for our app, including device name and model number, so we can improve how we customize your experience with the Lalo app.

Additionally, the Lalo app may be aware of the precise location of the mobile device you use in order to help you calculate your location. However, we do not collect, store, or otherwise have access to this data, and this data is not linked with any of your other personal information that we do collect.

When do we collect information?
We collect information from you when you register on and use the Lalo app, sign up for our newsletter, communicate with us via email, Twitter, or contact us through our website or any other digital platform.

How do we protect your information?
We implement a variety of security measures when you enter or accesses your information to maintain the safety of your personal information.

Our mobile application is scanned on a regular basis for security holes and known vulnerabilities to make your use of the Lalo app as safe as possible. Your personal information is contained behind secured networks and is only accessible by a limited number of persons who have special access rights to such systems and are required to keep the information confidential.

Furthermore, all payment transactions are processed through a gateway provider and are not stored or processed on our servers.

Do we use “cookies”?
Cookies are small files that a mobile application or its service provider transfers to your device’s hard drive through your device (if you allow) that enables the application’s or service provider’s systems to recognize your app and capture and remember certain information. While Lemur Labs does not use cookies on or through the Lalo app, our third-party analytics providers may. For more information on third parties, see the How We Share Your Information section of this Privacy Policy.

How We Follow Privacy Best Practices

California Online Privacy Protection Act of 2003 ( CalOPPA )
CalOPPA is the first state law in the United States to require commercial websites and online services to post a privacy policy. The law’s reach stretches well beyond California to require any person or company in the country (and conceivably the world) that operates a website collecting personally identifiable information from California consumers to post a conspicuous privacy policy on its website stating exactly the information being collected and those individuals with whom it is being shared, and to comply with this policy. See more here.

According to CalOPPA we agree to the following:

However, our third-party providers may track users. You can learn how to opt out of tracking by Google Analyticshere. You can learn how to opt out of Flurry’s tracking here.
